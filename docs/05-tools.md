# Tools

Status: Published HTML content. Last reviewed: 2026-07-13 13:53 BST.

## Introduction

This chapter lists the tools, consumables, and workspace items needed to assemble the PC without damaging components or losing small parts.

Retailer links are Amazon UK convenience links. Confirm current stock, seller, price, and exact product contents before ordering.

## Purpose

Prepare the correct tools before opening component packaging so the build can proceed without improvising around missing screwdrivers, ESD protection, lighting, or cable-management supplies.

## Estimated Time

15-25 minutes to gather and inspect tools.

## Difficulty

Beginner.

## Warnings

- Do not use a power drill on PC screws.
- Do not overtighten motherboard, cooler, M.2, or radiator screws.
- Do not use magnetic parts trays for loose storage drives or magnetic media. Normal PC screws and modern SSDs are fine, but keep the tray away from packaging labels and cards.
- Do not cut cable ties while pulling against a cable.
- Do not place the motherboard on carpet, fabric, or a bare metal table.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Put the Phillips #2 screwdriver, precision screwdriver, anti-static strap, screw tray, side cutters, and cable ties on the work surface.
2. Check that the Phillips #2 tip fits a spare case screw cleanly.
3. Check that the precision bit fits an M.2 screw or motherboard accessory screw.
4. Put the anti-static wrist strap on your wrist and confirm the alligator clip can reach the case chassis or another grounded metal point.
5. Divide the screw tray into labelled areas for motherboard screws, PSU screws, radiator screws, M.2 screws, and case panels.
6. Keep the Windows 11 Pro USB installer, keyboard, mouse, and display cable nearby for first boot.
7. Keep component manuals within reach, especially the motherboard and case manuals.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Phillips #2 screwdriver fits case and PSU screws.
- [ ] Precision screwdriver fits M.2 screws.
- [ ] Anti-static wrist strap is available.
- [ ] Screw tray or labelled containers are ready.
- [ ] Cable ties or reusable straps are ready.
- [ ] Side cutters are available for final cable cleanup.
- [ ] Windows 11 Pro USB installer is available.
- [ ] Keyboard, mouse, monitor, and display cable are available.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting with only a precision screwdriver and then struggling with case screws.
- Losing the M.2 screw or standoff.
- Using pliers on motherboard standoffs without checking whether the case already has the correct layout.
- Trimming cable ties before confirming that cables are routed correctly.
- Forgetting a keyboard before first boot.

## Expected Result

All required tools are present, checked, and staged before any hardware is removed from protective packaging.
